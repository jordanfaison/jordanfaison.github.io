Autonomous Vehicle: MIT-racecar

We will design and implement perception and planning algorithms for cars that can quickly navigate through complex environments. The class will be divided into three teams. Each team will be given one RC race car, powered by an NVIDIA Jetson TX-1 embedded supercomputer as well as an inertial measurement unit, a visual odometer, a laser scanner, and a camera. We will learn not only the basics of perception and planning algorithms, but we will also show the participants how to run the Robot Operating System (ROS) on the NVIDIA platform while interfacing with sensors and actuators. At the end of the course, we will race through the Florida Polytechnic IST to determine the winning team!

UPDATE: after several months working with the car, our final exam was a success, we were featured on local news, and look forward to helping future classes as they progress and learn how to interface their car. This class allowed us to develop algorithms, with real world applications, pathing the way for future progress.

RAD Makerspace Lab

The Rapid Application Development (RAD) Makerspace Lab is a hybrid manufacturing systems environment with 55 3D printers and digital object scanners, making it one of the largest MakerBot™ Innovation Centers in the US. The MakerBot™ Innovation Center has the full range of fifth-generation product technology, including the Mini Compact 3D Printer and Z18 3D Printer networked on a management platform for a seamless software solution. As an intern my job is to allow students to use what they learn in thew class and apply that to a functional real world prototype. My duties involve servicing and upkeep of all 55+ printers in the lab, as well as keep the lab current with new technologies and bring those ideas into the lab.
